The most interesting facts of Nepal:The Pride of Nepal


Mt.Everest [Sagarmatha]

Mount Everest is the world’s highest mountain above sea level. The official elevation of Mount Everest is 29,029 feet (8848 meters) above sea level. Mount Everest is located on the northern part of Nepal. The Mount Everest is shaped like a pyramid due to its three flat sides. Mount Everest was named in 1865, after Sir George Everest the Surveyor General of India. Before that, it used to be known as Peak XV by the British Government. Sherpa is an ethnic group indigenous to the mountainous region of Nepal and the Himalayas. Sherpa’s are considered as elite mountaineers, they work as climbing guides for foreigners. In 1953, Edmund Hillary (New Zealand) and Tenzing Norgay (Nepal) became the first climber to have successfully climbed Mount Everest.

Lumbini the birthplace of Lord Buddha

The Lord Gautam Buddha was born to a royal family in 623 BC in Lumbini, Nepal. His father was King Shuddhodan, and his mother was Queen Maya. His mother died soon after he was born, so he was raised by a stepmother Mahaprajapati. In his childhood, he was also called Siddharta.

Kathmandu:The Capital City Of Nepal

Kathmandu, also spelled Katmandu or Kantipur, capital of Nepal. It lies in a hilly region near the confluence of the Baghmati and Vishnumati rivers, at an elevation of 4,344 feet (1,324 metres) above sea level. It was founded in 723 by Raja Gunakamadeva.Kathmandu is the capital and largest city of Nepal, with a population of around 1 million. Also known as the city of temples.Kathmandu is for sure one of the best destinations for a spiritual sojourn and religious rejuvenation. The culture that exists since long before is a call for get-up-and-go. Explore! The medieval temples, shrines, and stupas in Kathmandu are a powerhouse of matchless elegance.
